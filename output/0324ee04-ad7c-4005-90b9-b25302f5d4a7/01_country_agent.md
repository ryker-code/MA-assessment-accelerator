### Currency & Capital
India allows repatriation of foreign exchange under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), provided profits are reinvested in India for a specified period. Restrictions may apply to certain sectors.

### Risk Flags
- Moderate inflationary pressures
- Potential regulatory changes in telecom sector
- Currency repatriation restrictions for foreign investors

### Narrative Summary
India's economy has shown resilience with GDP growth averaging around 4% over the past five years, though growth has moderated in recent years due to factors like election-related delays in public spending. Inflation remains moderate at 4.5%, supported by central bank interventions. The Indian Rupee (INR) has maintained stability despite global volatility, though the de facto exchange rate regime is classified as stabilized. Political stability scores indicate moderate risks, with ease of doing business ranked 108/190. Corporate tax rates at 29% are competitive but higher than some regional peers. Currency repatriation laws permit profit transfers under FEMA, though sector-specific restrictions exist. Key risks include inflationary pressures, regulatory shifts in the telecom sector (critical for Vodafone Idea), and potential currency controls impacting foreign investment returns.
