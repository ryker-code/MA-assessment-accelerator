### Narrative Summary
Israel's economy remains resilient, driven by its high-tech sector (17% of GDP) and strong export performance. However, inflation has remained elevated (3.2% in 2024) due to global and regional factors, with the Bank of Israel maintaining a cautious monetary policy. Political risks include ongoing judicial reforms that may impact governance stability. The corporate tax rate of 23% is competitive, and currency repatriation rules are generally favorable for foreign investors. While Israel ranks high in ease of doing business (25/190), geopolitical tensions and domestic policy debates pose potential headwinds for M&A activity.
