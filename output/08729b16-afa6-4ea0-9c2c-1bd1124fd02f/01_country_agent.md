### Currency & Capital
India allows repatriation of profits, dividends, and capital under Reserve Bank of India (RBI) regulations. Foreign investors must comply with FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) guidelines, which permit repatriation of earnings with prior approval for certain transactions.

### Risk Flags
- Inflationary pressures from global commodity prices
- Currency depreciation risks due to current account deficit
- Regulatory changes in the telecom sector
- Political instability in neighboring regions affecting trade

### Narrative Summary
India's economy has shown resilience despite global headwinds, with GDP growth rebounding post-pandemic but facing moderation in recent years. Inflation remains moderate at 4.5%, though external factors like oil prices and currency depreciation (INR has weakened against the USD) pose challenges. Political stability scores indicate governance risks, though India's ease of doing business rank has improved to 63/190 (2024). Corporate tax rates are standard at 29%, and currency repatriation is permitted under RBI oversight. Key risks include inflation volatility, currency trends, and sector-specific regulations impacting Vodafone Idea's operations. The telecom sector faces intense competition and regulatory scrutiny, which could affect valuations during an acquisition.
