### Narrative Summary
Pakistan's economy has shown moderate growth in recent years, with GDP expanding at 5.6% in 2024, though growth has slowed from previous peaks. However, the country faces structural challenges, including weak export performance, declining competitiveness, and a real exchange rate that has appreciated relative to productivity gains. Inflation remains a concern, though recent rates appear stable at around 4.5%. The political stability score reflects ongoing governance challenges, while the ease of doing business rank (108/190) indicates moderate regulatory hurdles. Corporate tax rates are relatively high at 29%, and currency repatriation laws, while not prohibitively restrictive, require careful navigation due to capital controls. Opportunities exist in digital infrastructure and telecommunications, aligning with Telenor Pakistan's operations, but macroeconomic volatility and policy uncertainty pose significant risks for foreign investors.
