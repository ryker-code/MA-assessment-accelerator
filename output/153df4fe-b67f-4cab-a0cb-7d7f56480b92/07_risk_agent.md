### Risk Details

#### Currency Translation and Earnings Drag
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** STC reports in SAR (pegged to USD), while Telenor Pakistan generates revenue in PKR. The country-agent confirms PKR is depreciating. This creates a significant FX translation risk where local growth in Pakistan may be completely offset by currency devaluation when consolidated into STC's financial statements.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement aggressive currency hedging strategies
  - Structure deal with a portion of payment in local currency to offset devaluation

#### Valuation Blind-Spot Risk
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** STC has a high acquisition capacity ($12B) and strategic urgency for 5G-Advanced expansion, but the company-agent failed to parse Telenor Pakistan's financials (0.0 values). Proceeding with a bid based on strategic fit without verified EBITDA/Net Debt creates a critical risk of overpayment or inheriting undisclosed liabilities.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Mandate a full financial audit and data room verification before any non-binding offer
  - Use earn-out structures based on verified performance milestones

#### Geopolitical Strategic Exposure
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC is a Saudi government-linked entity. Acquiring a strategic telecommunications asset in Pakistan (Political Stability Score: 35/100) ties STC's asset value directly to the volatile diplomatic relationship between Riyadh and Islamabad.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Secure political risk insurance (PRI)
  - Establish a local governance board with Pakistani nationals to buffer political friction

#### Macroeconomic Instability in Pakistan
- **Category:** GEOPOLITICAL
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Low political stability (35/100) and moderate ease of doing business rank (#108) create an environment of policy uncertainty that can impact spectrum licensing and operational continuity.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Diversify operational dependencies
  - Maintain high liquidity in local accounts to manage short-term shocks

#### Tax Leakage on Repatriation
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Significant disparity between Pakistan's corporate tax rate (29%) and Saudi Arabia's (20%) will lead to tax leakage during dividend repatriation unless specific tax treaties are optimized.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Utilize double taxation avoidance agreements (DTAA) between KSA and Pakistan

#### Integration Fatigue and Execution Risk
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC has acquired 19 companies since 2013. The sheer volume of prior acquisitions suggests a risk of integration fatigue or fragmented operational control, which could hinder the successful absorption of Telenor Pakistan.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Deploy a dedicated Post-Merger Integration (PMI) team specifically for the Pakistan market

#### Regulatory Approval Hurdles
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Both Saudi Arabian (SAMA) and Pakistani regulators may impose strict conditions on the transfer of a strategic telecom asset, potentially delaying the deal timeline.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Early engagement with the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) and SAMA

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the mismatch between STC's stable SAR-based reporting and Telenor Pakistan's depreciating PKR revenue, which could turn a locally profitable asset into a consolidated loss.
- There is a dangerous gap between STC's high capital readiness and the current lack of verified financial data for the target, creating a high probability of valuation error.
- The acquisition transforms a corporate investment into a geopolitical instrument, linking STC's balance sheet to Pakistan's low political stability score (35/100).
