### Narrative Summary
Canada's economy demonstrates resilience with a projected GDP growth of 1.5% in 2026, supported by stable political institutions and a high ease of doing business rank (13/190). While inflation peaked at 7.1% in 2022, it has since moderated to 3.2% in 2024, aligning with the Bank of Canada's inflation control measures. The Canadian dollar (CAD) has shown stability against the USD over the past five years, though 2023 saw temporary volatility due to global energy price fluctuations. Corporate tax rates remain competitive at 29% (federal + provincial), and foreign investors benefit from liberal currency repatriation laws. Risks include potential inflationary shocks from global commodity prices and exchange rate fluctuations tied to U.S. monetary policy. Canada's regulatory environment remains favorable for M&A activity, with strong legal protections and transparent governance frameworks.
