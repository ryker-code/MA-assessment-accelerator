### Risk Details

#### Dual-Jurisdiction National Security Deadlock
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The acquisition of Telesat (critical Canadian satellite infrastructure) by SpaceX (US defense contractor) triggers simultaneous reviews by CFIUS (USA) and Industry Canada. The overlap of two different national security regimes for the same strategic asset creates a high probability of deal blockage or onerous conditions.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Establish a joint regulatory task force
  - Offer government-backed guarantees on Canadian data sovereignty

#### IPO Timing and Integration Volatility
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** SpaceX is targeting a Nasdaq listing in June 2026. Executing a complex cross-border acquisition of Telesat simultaneously could introduce balance sheet volatility and management distraction, potentially negatively impacting the IPO valuation or delaying the listing.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Sequence the acquisition to close post-IPO
  - Utilize a separate SPV for the acquisition to isolate risk

#### LEO Spectrum and Regulatory Conflict
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Combining Starlink's constellation with Telesat's LEO ambitions may create spectrum interference issues and trigger antitrust scrutiny from the CRTC, as SpaceX would consolidate a dominant share of the Canadian satellite broadband market.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Proactive spectrum coordination with CRTC
  - Divestiture of overlapping non-core assets

#### Extreme Key-Man Dependency
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** SpaceX's strategic direction and valuation are heavily tied to Elon Musk. Any leadership instability or diversion of focus (e.g., to xAI or other ventures) could jeopardize the integration of Telesat.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement a robust succession plan
  - Appoint a dedicated CEO for the integrated satellite division

#### Canadian Regulatory Compliance (CRTC/PIPEDA)
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telesat operates under strict CRTC and PIPEDA (privacy) regulations. SpaceX's aggressive operational style may clash with Canada's more rigid telecommunications regulatory framework.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Retain local Canadian compliance leadership
  - Conduct a comprehensive gap analysis of data privacy protocols

#### Capital Intensity of Starship/LEO Programs
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The extreme capital requirements of the Starship program combined with the CapEx needed to scale Telesat's LEO infrastructure could strain SpaceX's cash flow despite Starlink's current profitability.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Secure long-term debt financing prior to acquisition
  - Leverage IPO proceeds for CapEx funding

#### CAD/USD Exchange Rate Volatility
- **Category:** CURRENCY
- **Severity:** LOW | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telesat's Canadian operations expose SpaceX to CAD/USD fluctuations, which could create earnings drag or increase the cost of local operational expenses.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement currency hedging strategies
  - Maintain CAD-denominated reserves for local operations

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The deal is not a simple commercial expansion but a geopolitical event, where the intersection of US (CFIUS) and Canadian (Industry Canada) security reviews creates a 'dual-gate' risk that outweighs financial due diligence.
- There is a critical timing conflict between the June 2026 IPO window and the integration timeline of a major foreign asset, creating a high-risk window for valuation volatility.
- The strategic synergy of combining Starlink and Telesat is offset by a significant regulatory risk regarding spectrum monopoly and antitrust concerns within the Canadian domestic market.
