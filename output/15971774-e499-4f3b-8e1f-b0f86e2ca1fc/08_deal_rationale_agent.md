## Deal Rationale & Recommendation

### Decision: ❌ **NO-GO**

**Strategic Fit Score:** 8.5/10

**Revisit Timeframe:** 6-12 months

### Top 5 Decision Rationale
1. The Risk Agent has assigned an overall rating of RED due to a 'Dual-Jurisdiction National Security Deadlock,' where simultaneous CFIUS and Industry Canada reviews create a high probability of deal blockage.
2. The timing of the acquisition conflicts with SpaceX's targeted June 2026 Nasdaq listing, introducing balance sheet volatility and management distraction that could negatively impact IPO valuation (Risk Agent).
3. Telesat's financial health is deteriorating, with Q1 2025 revenue decreasing by 23% to $117 million and a full-year 2025 net loss of $530 million (Valuation Research).
4. Despite high strategic fit, the potential for LEO spectrum and regulatory conflicts between Starlink and Telesat's existing authorizations presents a medium-to-high probability of operational friction (Risk Agent).
5. The acquisition would effectively fill SpaceX's gap in Canadian regional market access and enhance Direct-to-Device capabilities, but these synergies are outweighed by the current regulatory and timing risks (Buyer Sector Agent).

### Value Creation Avenues
- **NEW_CUSTOMERS** 🔼: Pairing SpaceX's Starlink scale with Telesat's established Canadian government and enterprise contract base to penetrate the public sector. _(requires integration)_
- **REVENUE_SYNERGY** 🔼: Integrating Telesat's spectrum authorizations to accelerate Starlink's Direct-to-Device (D2D) rollout in the Canadian market. _(requires integration)_
- **ASSET_MONETIZATION** ➡️: Leveraging Telesat's existing GEO infrastructure to provide hybrid LEO/GEO services, filling the gap in high-capacity stable links for industrial clients. _(requires integration)_

### Valuation
- **EV/EBITDA Range:** 6.0x – 9.5x (median: 7.8x)
- **Implied Enterprise Value:** USD 900.0m – USD 1425.0m

### Conditions for Reversal
- Successful completion and stabilization of the SpaceX IPO on Nasdaq.
- Obtaining a preliminary 'no-objection' or safe-harbor agreement from Industry Canada regarding national security and data sovereignty.
- Stabilization of Telesat's quarterly revenue growth to avoid acquiring a rapidly declining asset.

### Recommended Next Steps
- Postpone formal bid until post-IPO stabilization (Q3/Q4 2026).
- Initiate informal regulatory sounding with the CRTC and Industry Canada to gauge appetite for a US-led acquisition of critical satellite infrastructure.
- Conduct a detailed technical audit of Telesat's spectrum authorizations to ensure they are compatible with Starlink's current LEO architecture.
