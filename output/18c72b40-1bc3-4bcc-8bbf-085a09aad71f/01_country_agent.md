### Narrative Summary
Canada's economy has shown resilience over the past five years, with GDP growth recovering strongly post-pandemic. While inflation peaked in 2022 due to global supply chain disruptions, it has since moderated to 3.2% in 2024, aligning with central bank targets. The Canadian dollar (CAD) has remained stable against the USD, reflecting strong institutional frameworks and trade relationships. Politically, Canada maintains high stability with a robust governance score and is ranked among the top countries for ease of doing business. Corporate tax rates are competitive at 29%, with clear rules for profit repatriation. Opportunities abound in Canada's tech-driven economy, though buyers should monitor potential risks from global trade tensions and energy sector volatility. Overall, the country presents a favorable environment for M&A activity in sectors like telecommunications and aerospace.
