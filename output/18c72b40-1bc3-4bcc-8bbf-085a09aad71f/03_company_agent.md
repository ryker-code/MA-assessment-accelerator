### Narrative Summary
Telesat is currently undergoing a critical strategic transition from traditional geostationary (GEO) satellite operations to a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) constellation known as 'Lightspeed'. This pivot is supported by substantial government backing, including $2.54 billion in funding from the Canadian and Quebec governments, positioning the company as a key player in Canada's space economy. 

However, the company's financial health is under pressure. Legacy revenue streams are eroding rapidly, with 2025 quarterly revenues showing declines of 23% to 30% compared to 2024. The company has shifted from a strong net income position in 2023 to significant net losses in 2024 and 2025, exacerbated by asset impairments and FX headwinds. The company's valuation and future viability are now heavily contingent on the successful deployment and commercialization of the Lightspeed network.
