### Risk Details

#### Government Funding Clawback Risk
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Telesat has secured $2.54 billion in funding from the Government of Canada and Quebec for the Lightspeed constellation. These funds are typically contingent on Canadian ownership or operational control. A takeover by SpaceX (a US entity) could trigger immediate repayment requirements or the cancellation of future funding tranches, creating a massive capital hole.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Negotiate a 'Canadian Subsidiary' structure to maintain local control
  - Seek prior approval from Canadian federal and provincial governments

#### Strategic Redundancy and Antitrust Conflict
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** SpaceX's primary growth engine is Starlink (LEO). Telesat is pivoting to Lightspeed (LEO). Combining these creates a near-monopoly in LEO broadband, which will likely trigger aggressive antitrust reviews in both the US and Canada, potentially forcing the divestiture of the very LEO assets SpaceX intends to acquire.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Position the acquisition as a GEO-to-LEO transition play rather than LEO expansion
  - Offer spectrum sharing agreements to competitors

#### Severe Revenue Erosion
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Telesat is experiencing a catastrophic revenue decline, dropping from $820M in 2021 to a projected $432M in 2025. This indicates a failing legacy GEO business that may be deteriorating faster than the LEO pivot can compensate.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Aggressive cost-cutting of legacy GEO operations
  - Accelerated deployment of Lightspeed to capture new revenue streams

#### CFIUS and National Security Blockage
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The acquisition of critical satellite infrastructure and Arctic orbit access by a US company may trigger intense scrutiny from both CFIUS (USA) and the Investment Canada Act, especially given the sensitive nature of NORAD collaboration mentioned in buyer research.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Establish a national security agreement (NSA) with the Canadian government
  - Ensure data sovereignty for Canadian government traffic

#### LEO Transition Execution Risk
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telesat's entire valuation is now predicated on the 'Lightspeed' pivot. Any technical failure or delay in the LEO constellation deployment would leave the company with negative net profits and no viable core business.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Leverage SpaceX's superior launch capabilities to accelerate deployment
  - Diversify satellite hardware providers

#### FX Volatility on USD Debt
- **Category:** CURRENCY
- **Severity:** LOW | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telesat carries significant USD-denominated debt while operating in a Canadian context; fluctuations in the CAD/USD exchange rate could impact debt servicing costs.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement currency hedging strategies
  - Refinance debt through SpaceX's US-based capital structures

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the tension between Telesat's reliance on Canadian government subsidies and SpaceX's status as a US-based private entity, which could invalidate billions in funding.
- There is a significant strategic overlap between Starlink and Lightspeed; the acquisition may be viewed by regulators as a 'killer acquisition' to eliminate a LEO competitor rather than a synergistic merger.
- While SpaceX has the technical capability to fix Telesat's LEO deployment issues, the financial drag of Telesat's collapsing legacy revenue creates a valuation challenge that cannot be solved by technical synergy alone.
