## Deal Rationale & Recommendation

### Decision: ❌ **NO-GO**

**Strategic Fit Score:** 8.0/10

**Revisit Timeframe:** 6-12 months

### Top 5 Decision Rationale
1. Critical financial instability: Telesat reported a severe net loss of $530 million in 2025 and a consistent revenue decline reaching -25.6% YoY in 2025.
2. High capital risk: The $2.54 billion in funding from the Government of Canada and Quebec for the Lightspeed constellation is likely contingent on Canadian ownership, creating a massive clawback risk upon acquisition by a US entity.
3. Antitrust barriers: Combining Starlink and Lightspeed would create a near-monopoly in LEO broadband, triggering high-probability antitrust blocks in both the US and Canada.
4. Regulatory hurdles: The acquisition of sensitive Canadian telecom infrastructure would trigger intensive CFIUS and Canadian national security reviews, as noted in the buyer-country assessment.
5. Operational mismatch: While the strategic fit is high (addressing SpaceX's 20% satellite services penetration gap), the target's current revenue erosion and debt profile make it a distressed asset with prohibitive regulatory baggage.

### Value Creation Avenues
- **NEW_CUSTOMERS** 🔼: Leverage Telesat's $1.2 billion contractual backlog to immediately expand SpaceX's government and enterprise client base in Canada. _(requires integration)_
- **REVENUE_SYNERGY** 🔼: Combine Telesat's Arctic orbit access with Starlink's LEO constellation to achieve total global coverage and dominate high-latitude connectivity. _(requires integration)_
- **ASSET_MONETIZATION** ➡️: Utilize Telesat's existing spectrum authorizations to optimize Starlink's frequency allocation and reduce regulatory friction for new launches.

### Valuation
- **EV/EBITDA Range:** 6.0x – 9.5x (median: 7.8x)
- **Implied Enterprise Value:** USD 906.0m – USD 1434.5m

### Conditions for Reversal
- Written guarantee from Canadian/Quebec governments that the $2.54bn funding will not be clawed back under US ownership.
- Successful pre-clearance from antitrust regulators regarding LEO broadband market concentration.
- Stabilization of Telesat's revenue decline to less than 5% YoY.

### Recommended Next Steps
- Conduct deep-dive legal due diligence on the specific terms of the Lightspeed government funding agreements.
- Initiate informal 'soft-sounding' discussions with the CRTC and Canadian federal government regarding ownership changes.
- Explore a minority investment or strategic partnership instead of a full acquisition to mitigate antitrust and clawback risks.
