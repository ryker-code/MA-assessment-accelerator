### Currency & Capital
Pakistan allows repatriation of foreign exchange earnings under the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act (FERA), but requires approval from the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) for large transactions. Capital controls may apply to certain sectors.

### Risk Flags
- High inflation (7.2% in 2024)
- Currency depreciation (PKR/USD exchange rate rising)
- Political instability risks
- Fiscal consolidation measures impacting growth
- Capital controls and repatriation restrictions

### Narrative Summary
Pakistan's economy has shown mixed performance over the past five years, with GDP growth recovering to 2.5% in 2024 amid IMF-supported reforms. However, inflation remains elevated at 7.2%, and the Pakistani Rupee (PKR) has depreciated against the USD, increasing import costs. Political stability remains a concern, reflected in a low political stability score of 35.0. The ease of doing business rank of 108 indicates moderate regulatory challenges. Corporate tax rates at 29% are relatively high, and while repatriation of profits is permitted, capital controls and SBP approvals add complexity for foreign investors. The IMF's Extended Fund Facility (EFF) program aims to stabilize the economy, but high interest rates and fiscal austerity measures continue to weigh on growth and poverty reduction.
