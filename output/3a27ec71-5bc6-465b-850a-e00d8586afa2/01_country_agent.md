### Narrative Summary
Canada maintains a stable macroeconomic environment with moderate GDP growth projections and low inflation. The country's political stability and high ease of doing business ranking make it an attractive M&A destination. However, currency repatriation risks are minimal due to open capital account policies. The corporate tax rate of 29% is standard for multinational operations. Inflation remains controlled at 2.5%, supported by prudent fiscal policies. While the Canadian dollar has shown stability against major currencies, global geopolitical risks could introduce volatility. Regulatory frameworks for foreign investment are well-established, though sector-specific regulations (e.g., telecommunications) may require careful compliance review. Overall, Canada presents a low-risk, high-potential market for acquiring Telesat, with strong institutional frameworks and economic resilience.
