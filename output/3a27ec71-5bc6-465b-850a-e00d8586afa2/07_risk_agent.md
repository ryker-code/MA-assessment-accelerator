### Risk Details

#### Antitrust and Market Dominance Blockage
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** SpaceX already holds a 45% global market share in aerospace. Combining this dominance with Telesat's established Canadian satellite infrastructure is likely to trigger aggressive antitrust reviews by both the CRTC (Canada) and US regulators, potentially leading to a deal block or forced divestitures.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Proactive engagement with CRTC
  - Offering open-access commitments for satellite capacity

#### Regulatory Sovereignty vs. US Tech Control
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Conflict between Canada's Telecommunications Act (which protects critical infrastructure) and US ITAR/export controls on SpaceX's aerospace technology. The requirement for Canadian operational control may clash with SpaceX's vertically integrated, US-centric security protocols.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Establishing a ring-fenced Canadian subsidiary
  - Negotiating specific security clearances with the Canadian government

#### Critical Financial Data Gap
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The company-agent failed to parse Telesat's financial data (Revenue, EBITDA, Net Debt all reported as 0.0). This creates a massive valuation risk where SpaceX may be operating on outdated or incorrect assumptions regarding the target's leverage and cash flow.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Immediate manual audit of SEC filings
  - Requesting a certified financial statement from the target

#### Key-Man Dependency Risk
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** SpaceX's strategic direction and valuation are heavily tied to Elon Musk. Any volatility associated with his leadership or the upcoming 2026 IPO could destabilize the acquisition financing or strategic integration of Telesat.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Strengthening the SpaceX executive leadership tier
  - Implementing a robust corporate governance framework post-IPO

#### Technology Cannibalization
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** SpaceX's Starlink (LEO) may fundamentally cannibalize the value of Telesat's legacy satellite assets. There is a risk that the acquisition price is based on legacy revenue streams that will vanish rapidly post-integration.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Structuring the deal with earn-outs based on legacy asset performance
  - Accelerating the migration of Telesat customers to Starlink

#### Canadian Telecom Regulatory Compliance
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telesat must comply with PIPEDA and CRTC regulations. SpaceX's aggressive operational style and data-handling practices may conflict with Canada's strict privacy and consumer protection laws.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Appointing a dedicated Canadian Compliance Officer
  - Updating data privacy protocols to meet PIPEDA standards

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The primary deal-breaker is the intersection of SpaceX's existing 45% global market share and Canada's protective stance on critical telecommunications infrastructure, creating a high-probability antitrust barrier.
- A dangerous blind spot exists where SpaceX's massive acquisition capacity ($14bn) meets a total lack of verified financial data for Telesat, risking significant overpayment.
- There is a fundamental tension between US aerospace export controls (ITAR) and the Canadian regulatory requirement for domestic oversight of telecom networks.
