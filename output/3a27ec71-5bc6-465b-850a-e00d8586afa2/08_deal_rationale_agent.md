## Deal Rationale & Recommendation

### Decision: ❌ **NO-GO**

**Strategic Fit Score:** 8.5/10

**Revisit Timeframe:** 6-12 months

### Top 5 Decision Rationale
1. Despite a high strategic fit, the overall risk rating is RED due to SpaceX's 45% global market share, which makes an antitrust block by the CRTC highly probable (Risk Agent/Buyer Sector Agent).
2. There is a critical conflict between Canada's Telecommunications Act regarding critical infrastructure and US ITAR/export controls on SpaceX technology, creating a high-severity regulatory barrier (Risk Agent).
3. Telesat's financial trajectory is severely declining, with a 23% revenue decrease in Q1 2025 and a net loss of $530 million for the full year 2025 (Valuation Research).
4. The acquisition would directly serve SpaceX's priority of 'global dominance in satellite-based internet services' by securing Telesat's valuable spectrum authorizations (Buyer Company Agent/Valuation Research).
5. SpaceX possesses massive acquisition capacity ($14,000m), but the target's operational instability and regulatory sensitivity outweigh the financial ease of the transaction (Buyer Company Agent/Risk Agent).

### Value Creation Avenues
- **ASSET_MONETIZATION** 🔼: Pairing SpaceX's Starlink deployment speed with Telesat's existing spectrum authorizations to bypass years of regulatory filing in Canada. _(requires integration)_
- **NEW_CUSTOMERS** ➡️: Leveraging Telesat's established Canadian government and enterprise relationships to accelerate Starlink's B2B and B2G penetration in North America. _(requires integration)_
- **COST_SYNERGY** 🔼: Replacing Telesat's expensive legacy satellite replacement cycle with SpaceX's low-cost, fully reusable launch architecture. _(requires integration)_

### Valuation
- **EV/EBITDA Range:** 6.0x – 9.5x (median: 7.8x)
- **Implied Enterprise Value:** USD 900.0m – USD 1425.0m

### Conditions for Reversal
- Obtaining a preliminary 'no-objection' letter from the CRTC regarding market concentration.
- Establishment of a legally viable 'ring-fenced' Canadian operational entity to satisfy sovereignty requirements while maintaining US tech control.
- Stabilization of Telesat's quarterly revenue to prevent further valuation erosion.

### Recommended Next Steps
- Initiate informal regulatory sounding with the Canadian government to assess the feasibility of a US-owned critical satellite infrastructure.
- Conduct a detailed spectrum audit to determine if the authorizations can be acquired via a targeted asset purchase rather than a full corporate acquisition to mitigate antitrust risk.
- Perform a deep-dive financial due diligence to identify the root cause of the 23% revenue decline.
