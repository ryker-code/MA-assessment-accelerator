### Narrative Summary
The United States maintains a robust and resilient economy with a nominal GDP of $26.9 trillion in 2023, supported by strong institutional frameworks and innovation-driven sectors. GDP growth has rebounded from pandemic-induced contractions, though projections indicate moderate expansion (1.5% in 2024) amid global economic headwinds. Inflation remains subdued at 3.5%, and the U.S. dollar retains its status as the global reserve currency, ensuring stable capital flows. Political stability is high (score: 90/100), with a business-friendly environment ranked 12th globally. Corporate tax rates are competitive at 21% federal, though state-level taxes may increase effective rates. Foreign investors benefit from unrestricted currency repatriation, though risks include regulatory changes in tech sectors and potential trade policy volatility. Overall, the U.S. offers a favorable environment for M&A activity, though due diligence should account for sector-specific regulatory dynamics.
