### Narrative Summary
The U.S. data storage market, particularly the external controller-based segment, experienced robust growth in 2024, reaching $22.1 billion with a 5.4% growth rate. Key players like HPE, NetApp, and Dell dominate the market, though competition remains fragmented. Regulatory risks are moderate, driven by data privacy laws and environmental standards for data centers. Technological disruption is moderate, with cloud migration and AI-driven demand reshaping storage needs. Sector-specific KPIs such as storage density and cost per GB are critical for evaluating competitive positioning. Risks include reliance on partial market data and evolving regulatory requirements.
