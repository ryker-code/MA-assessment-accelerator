### Currency & Capital
The U.S. allows unrestricted repatriation of foreign earnings, though companies may face U.S. federal and state taxes on repatriated profits. No significant capital controls exist.

### Risk Flags
- High corporate tax rates relative to global peers
- Potential regulatory scrutiny for tech sector acquisitions

### Narrative Summary
The United States maintains a robust and stable macroeconomic environment, with strong GDP growth resilience despite recent moderation. The USD remains the world's primary reserve currency, benefiting from deep financial markets and low volatility. Political stability is high, supported by institutional strength, though polarization poses long-term governance risks. The U.S. ranks among the top countries for ease of doing business, with a well-developed legal framework. Corporate tax rates are competitive globally, though state-level variations and repatriation tax considerations may impact M&A valuations. Regulatory scrutiny in the tech sector could introduce transactional complexity. Overall, the U.S. offers a favorable environment for foreign investment with manageable risks.
