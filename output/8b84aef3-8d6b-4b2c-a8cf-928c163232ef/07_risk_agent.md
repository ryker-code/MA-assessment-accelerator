### Risk Details

#### Extreme Financial Scale Mismatch
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The buyer's total revenue ($11.5B) is significantly smaller than the target's revenue ($62B). Furthermore, the buyer's estimated acquisition capacity ($4.65B) is insufficient to cover even a fraction of the target's net debt ($45B), making a full acquisition financially impossible without massive external financing or a complete restructuring of the deal into a carve-out.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Pivot from full acquisition to a strategic carve-out of IBM's AI software unit
  - Explore a joint venture rather than a merger

#### Unsustainable Post-Deal Leverage
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Combining IBM's 3.0x EBITDA leverage with Booz Allen's 2.6x leverage, while assuming IBM's $45B net debt, would likely push the combined entity's leverage to levels that would trigger credit downgrades and breach debt covenants, especially given the buyer's smaller EBITDA base ($1.3B).
- **Mitigants:**
  - Equity-heavy deal structure
  - Divestiture of IBM's declining infrastructure assets prior to closing

#### Federal Monopoly Regulatory Scrutiny
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Combining Booz Allen's dominant position in US Federal consulting with IBM's enterprise AI and infrastructure capabilities could create a 'too-big-to-fail' entity in the government tech space, triggering intense antitrust scrutiny from US regulators regarding government contract competition.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Proactive engagement with FTC/DOJ
  - Commitments to open-standard AI frameworks

#### Legacy Pension Liability Exposure
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** IBM has significant unfunded pension liabilities (Excess PPP and Retention Plan) that could create long-term cash flow drains and balance sheet volatility for the acquirer.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Pension risk transfer to insurance companies
  - Negotiating a liability carve-out

#### Revenue Concentration Risk
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Booz Allen Hamilton has a high concentration of revenue from the US Federal Government, making the combined entity highly sensitive to US federal budget cuts or shifts in political administration.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Leveraging IBM's commercial software client base to diversify revenue

#### Legacy Segment Contraction
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** IBM's Infrastructure (-7.6%) and Consulting (-2.0%) segments are in decline, which may drag down the overall growth profile of the combined entity despite AI software growth.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Aggressive divestiture of legacy hardware assets
  - Accelerated migration of consulting clients to AI-driven models

#### Tech Sector Antitrust Environment
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** General high regulatory scrutiny for tech sector acquisitions in the USA may lead to prolonged approval timelines or required divestitures.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Detailed antitrust pre-filing analysis

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the massive financial asymmetry; Booz Allen is fundamentally too small to acquire IBM in its entirety, as the target's debt alone exceeds the buyer's total enterprise value.
- There is a strong strategic fit in AI capabilities, but the financial execution risk is extreme due to the combined leverage of two already leveraged companies.
- The deal transforms from a standard acquisition into a high-risk regulatory event because it merges a dominant federal consultant with a global tech giant, potentially creating a monopoly in government AI services.
