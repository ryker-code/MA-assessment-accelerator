### Narrative Summary
Israel maintains a resilient economy driven by its high-tech sector, which contributes 17% of GDP and 56% of exports. GDP growth rebounded strongly post-pandemic (6.5% in 2022) but moderated to 2.5% in 2024, with the IMF projecting 3.5% growth for 2026. Inflation eased to 3.2% in December 2024, though above the Bank of Israel's 2-3% target. The corporate tax rate of 23% is competitive, and foreign investment is permitted with limited restrictions on capital repatriation. Political stability remains strong (World Bank score: 65/100), though ongoing judicial reforms and cost-of-living policies could introduce regulatory uncertainty. Israel ranks 108th globally for ease of doing business, with a stable currency (ILS) and robust financial markets.
