### Currency & Capital
India allows repatriation of profits and dividends under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), but requires compliance with documentation and approval for large transactions. Restrictions may apply to capital controls during periods of macroeconomic stress.

### Risk Flags
- Currency depreciation risks due to trade deficits and foreign exchange inflows
- High corporate tax rate relative to global peers
- Potential regulatory changes in foreign investment policies

### Narrative Summary
India has demonstrated resilience in GDP growth post-pandemic, with a diversified economy driven by services, manufacturing, and IT sectors. However, inflation remains a concern, with the latest rate at 4.5%, and the Indian Rupee (INR) has shown a depreciating trend against major currencies. Political stability scores indicate moderate risk, with ease of doing business ranked 108th globally. The corporate tax rate of 29% is relatively high, though recent reforms aim to improve investor confidence. Currency repatriation is permissible under FEMA but subject to regulatory scrutiny. Opportunities exist in India's growing digital economy and infrastructure sectors, though acquirers should monitor macroeconomic volatility and policy shifts.
