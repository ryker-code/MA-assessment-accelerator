### Currency & Capital
Profit repatriation is permitted under Pakistani law, but requires approval from the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) and compliance with foreign exchange regulations. Capital controls may apply during periods of economic instability.

### Risk Flags
- High inflation (7.2% in 2024)
- Currency depreciation (PKR weakened significantly against USD)
- Political instability and policy uncertainty
- Capital controls during economic crises

### Narrative Summary
Pakistan's economy has shown mixed performance over the past five years, with GDP growth fluctuating between contraction (-0.5% in 2020) and expansion (up to 6.1% in 2022), though growth has moderated to 2.5% in 2024. The IMF's Extended Fund Facility (EFF) program has helped stabilize the economy, but headline inflation remains elevated at 7.2% (2024), and the Pakistani Rupee (PKR) has depreciated significantly against the USD. Political instability and regulatory challenges persist, though the ease of doing business has improved slightly. The corporate tax rate of 29% is standard, but foreign investors must navigate capital controls and currency repatriation requirements. Opportunities exist in sectors like telecommunications, but risks include inflationary pressures, currency volatility, and potential policy shifts.
