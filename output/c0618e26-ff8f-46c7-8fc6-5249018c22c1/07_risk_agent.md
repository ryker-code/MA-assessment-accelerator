### Risk Details

#### Vertical Foreclosure and Antitrust Blockage
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** T-Mobile (Buyer) is a primary customer of American Tower (Target). If T-Mobile acquires the infrastructure, it creates a vertical integration that could allow T-Mobile to disadvantage competitors (Verizon, AT&T) by restricting tower access or increasing lease costs. This is highly likely to trigger aggressive DOJ/FTC antitrust intervention.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Structural remedies (divestiture of specific tower clusters)
  - Binding 'Open Access' agreements for competitors

#### Post-Deal Leverage Sustainability
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** T-Mobile currently carries $45B in net debt. American Tower, as a REIT, typically operates with high leverage to fund asset acquisition. Combining these balance sheets may push the consolidated Debt/EBITDA ratio beyond investment-grade thresholds, risking credit rating downgrades.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Equity issuance to fund the acquisition
  - Aggressive deleveraging plan post-integration

#### REIT Tax Structure Conflict
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** American Tower operates as a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT), which requires specific distributions to shareholders to avoid corporate tax. T-Mobile is a standard C-Corp. Integrating a REIT into a C-Corp typically triggers significant tax liabilities or requires a complex restructuring of the target's legal entity.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Maintaining American Tower as a separate subsidiary
  - Tax-efficient spin-off of non-core assets

#### Technological Obsolescence (Satellite/Open RAN)
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** The rise of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellations and Open RAN architectures may reduce the long-term reliance on traditional macro-tower infrastructure, potentially eroding the terminal value of the target's assets.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Diversification into edge computing and small cell hosting
  - Investment in satellite-ground station infrastructure

#### FCC Regulatory Volatility
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Ongoing uncertainty regarding net neutrality and spectrum allocation policies by the FCC could impact the utilization rates of tower infrastructure and the buyer's ability to deploy 5G services efficiently.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Active government relations and lobbying
  - Flexible network architecture

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the transition from a customer-vendor relationship to an owner-operator model in a highly concentrated triopoly market, which creates an immediate antitrust red flag.
- There is a fundamental financial mismatch between T-Mobile's operational telco balance sheet and American Tower's capital-intensive REIT balance sheet, which could jeopardize the buyer's credit rating.
- While the acquisition solves T-Mobile's strategic gap in infrastructure control, it introduces significant regulatory and tax complexities that are not present in standard horizontal mergers.
