### Risk Details

#### FX Translation Drag on Consolidated Earnings
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Telenor Pakistan shows strong local currency EBITDA growth (+17%) but negative USD-denominated growth (-2%) due to PKR devaluation. Since STC reports in SAR (pegged to USD), the operational gains in Pakistan will be largely erased upon consolidation, creating a persistent earnings drag.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement aggressive local currency hedging strategies
  - Price adjustments to ARPU to offset inflation

#### Capital Repatriation and 'Trapped Cash' Risk
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC possesses massive acquisition capacity ($6.2bn cash), but Pakistan's State Bank (SBP) maintains strict oversight and approval requirements for large foreign exchange transactions. There is a high risk that dividends generated by Telenor Pakistan cannot be efficiently repatriated to Saudi Arabia.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Negotiate specific repatriation guarantees with SBP as a condition of the deal
  - Reinvest profits locally into infrastructure to avoid repatriation hurdles

#### Strategic Vision vs. Operational Reality Gap
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC's 'Always Forward' 2025 strategy focuses on high-end digital enablement (AI, Cloud, Fintech). However, Telenor Pakistan is operating in a market with low ARPU ($5) and significant rural connectivity gaps, meaning STC may find it difficult to deploy its advanced digital stack in a market still struggling with basic infrastructure and macro volatility.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Phased rollout of digital services
  - Tailoring fintech offerings specifically for the unbanked Pakistani population

#### Severe PKR Currency Volatility
- **Category:** CURRENCY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The Pakistani Rupee is in a state of depreciation, which directly impacts the valuation of the asset and the USD-equivalent returns for the buyer.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Currency collars in the purchase agreement
  - Local currency financing for CapEx

#### Pakistan Political Instability
- **Category:** GEOPOLITICAL
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** A political stability score of 35/100 indicates a high risk of policy reversals, civil unrest, or regulatory shifts that could disrupt telecommunications operations.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Political risk insurance (PRI)
  - Maintaining strong local government relations

#### Regulatory Approval for Ownership Transfer
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** The divestment from Telenor ASA to STC requires PTA approval and adherence to spectrum trading frameworks, which may be subject to delays or conditional requirements.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Early engagement with PTA
  - Compliance audit against 2016 Spectrum Trading Framework

#### Low ARPU and Market Saturation
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** An ARPU of $5 limits the ability to rapidly increase margins without significant price hikes, which may be resisted in a high-inflation environment.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Upselling data packages
  - Expanding into B2B digital services

#### Saudi Government Strategic Dependency
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC's strategic direction is heavily tied to Saudi Vision 2030; any shift in the Kingdom's foreign investment priorities could impact the long-term support for the Pakistan venture.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Aligning the Pakistan acquisition explicitly with Vision 2030's international expansion goals

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the 'Growth Paradox': Telenor Pakistan is operationally successful in local terms (+17% EBITDA), but this success is invisible or negative to STC's consolidated USD/SAR financials due to PKR devaluation.
- There is a significant mismatch between STC's high-tech 'Digital Enabler' ambitions and the target's reality of managing low-ARPU rural connectivity in a volatile macro environment.
- STC's strong balance sheet provides a safety net for the acquisition cost, but it does not mitigate the 'trapped cash' risk created by Pakistan's SBP capital controls.
