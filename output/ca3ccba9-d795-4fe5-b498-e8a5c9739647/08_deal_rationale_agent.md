### Top 5 Decision Rationale
1. Financial Overleverage: Safeway's current net debt of $6.8B results in a 2.4x EBITDA leverage ratio, which is already at the ceiling of its 2.3x-2.5x target range, making the acquisition of a premium asset financially unsustainable.
2. Critical Risk Rating: The Risk Agent assigned an overall RED rating, citing a high probability of unsustainable post-deal leverage and potential antitrust blockage by the FTC.
3. Integration Track Record: Safeway has a documented history of 'integration challenges' from its 2014 Albertsons merger, increasing the likelihood of synergy erosion when merging with a high-culture premium brand like Whole Foods.
4. Information Asymmetry: The Company Agent reported a confidence score of only 30% due to LLM parsing failures, creating a critical lack of verified financial data for the target company.
5. Valuation Gap: Based on comparable retail multiples (6.0x-9.5x), the implied valuation of Whole Foods would likely exceed Safeway's estimated acquisition capacity of $9.79B.
