### Currency & Capital
The UK allows free repatriation of profits and foreign exchange without restrictions, as per the Foreign Exchange Control Act 1994 and the UK's status as a major global financial hub.

### Risk Flags

### Narrative Summary
The United Kingdom maintains a stable macroeconomic environment with a resilient financial sector, though growth has moderated post-pandemic. GDP growth averaged 2.5% in 2024, supported by services and investment, though challenges include public sector strains and labor market constraints. Inflation has eased to 3.2% in 2024 after peaking at 7.0% in 2022, with the Bank of England managing monetary policy amid global rate cuts. The GBP has shown stability against the USD, with no major capital controls hindering foreign investment. Corporate tax rates remain competitive at 19%, and the UK ranks 12th globally for ease of doing business. Risks include ongoing post-Brexit regulatory complexity, healthcare system pressures, and potential labor shortages impacting sectors like telecoms. The UK's regulatory framework and financial infrastructure present opportunities for foreign investors, though due diligence should consider sector-specific challenges.
