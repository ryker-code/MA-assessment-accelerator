### Currency & Capital
The U.S. allows unrestricted repatriation of foreign earnings, though corporate profits are subject to federal and state income taxes. No capital controls or restrictions on currency outflows exist.

### Risk Flags
- High corporate tax complexity
- Potential regulatory changes under new administrations

### Narrative Summary
The U.S. economy demonstrates resilience with moderate GDP growth over the past five years, though recent projections indicate a slight slowdown in 2024. Inflation has eased to 3.7%, aligning with Federal Reserve targets, and the dollar remains stable against major currencies. Political stability is high, supported by institutional strength, though regulatory shifts under changing administrations pose potential risks. The corporate tax rate of 21% (federal) is competitive globally, but state-level variations and complex compliance requirements may affect foreign investors. The U.S. ranks 12th in ease of doing business, reflecting a favorable regulatory environment for M&A activity. Currency repatriation is unrestricted, facilitating capital mobility for foreign investors.
