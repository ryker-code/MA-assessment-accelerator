### Risk Details

#### FX Translation and Earnings Drag
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** STC reports in SAR (pegged to USD), while Telenor Pakistan generates revenue in PKR. With PKR in a state of depreciation and Pakistan facing 24% inflation, there is a high risk of significant earnings erosion when consolidating Telenor's financials into STC's group accounts.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement aggressive currency hedging strategies
  - Price adjustments in Telenor's consumer plans to offset inflation

#### Capital Repatriation Friction
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** The conflict between Saudi Arabia's outbound M&A requirements (CMA/Ministry approval) and Pakistan's strict State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) repatriation rules creates a risk where capital is 'trapped' in Pakistan, hindering STC's ability to realize dividends or exit capital.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Negotiate specific repatriation guarantees with the Board of Investment (BOI)
  - Utilize bilateral investment treaties between KSA and Pakistan

#### Regulatory Approval Timeline Mismatch
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** The deal requires simultaneous approval from the Saudi Capital Market Authority (CMA) and the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA). The complexity of spectrum trading frameworks in Pakistan combined with Saudi outbound scrutiny may extend the closing window beyond STC's strategic timeline.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Parallel filing of regulatory applications
  - Engagement of local lobbyists in both Riyadh and Islamabad

#### Macroeconomic Instability
- **Category:** GEOPOLITICAL
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** Pakistan's low political stability score (35/100) and high inflation (24%) create an unpredictable operating environment that could impair Telenor's operational viability and consumer purchasing power.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Diversification of revenue streams into digital services
  - Political risk insurance (PRI)

#### Currency Depreciation
- **Category:** CURRENCY
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** The ongoing depreciation of the PKR directly impacts the valuation of the asset and the cost of importing network equipment (CapEx) which is typically USD-denominated.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Local sourcing of infrastructure components
  - USD-linked pricing for corporate contracts

#### Spectrum and Regulatory Complexity
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Complexity surrounding the Spectrum Trading Framework (2016) and PTA compliance may lead to unexpected costs or delays in network upgrades.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Comprehensive regulatory due diligence on current spectrum licenses
  - Direct engagement with PTA leadership

#### Infrastructure Resilience Gap
- **Category:** MARKET
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Telenor Pakistan requires significant investment in climate-resilient infrastructure, which may conflict with STC's immediate focus on high-end 5G and AI integration.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Phased CapEx plan prioritizing resilience before advanced tech deployment
  - Leveraging STC's global vendor partnerships for cost-effective resilient hardware

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the currency mismatch; STC's stable SAR reporting creates a stark contrast with Telenor's PKR-denominated earnings, making the deal highly sensitive to Pakistani macro-volatility.
- There is a significant 'regulatory pincer' effect where the deal must satisfy both Saudi outbound capital controls and Pakistani repatriation restrictions, potentially trapping liquidity.
- A strategic misalignment exists between STC's high-tech 5G/AI ambitions and the fundamental need for climate-resilient basic infrastructure in the Pakistani market.
