### Narrative Summary
Pakistan's economy has shown moderate growth over the past five years, with real GDP expanding at an average rate of ~6% annually from 2020 to 2022, followed by a slowdown to ~5.6% in 2023 and 2024. However, the country faces structural challenges, including weak export performance, declining competitiveness, and persistent inflationary pressures. The Pakistani Rupee has been depreciating against major currencies, exacerbated by fiscal imbalances and reliance on imports. Political stability remains a concern, with a governance score of 35/100 reflecting institutional weaknesses. While the ease of doing business rank (108/190) indicates room for improvement, the telecom sector—represented by Telenor Pakistan—benefits from growing digital adoption and government initiatives to expand digital infrastructure. Risks include currency volatility, capital controls, and regulatory uncertainties, which could impact foreign investment returns. Opportunities exist in leveraging Pakistan's young population and expanding mobile penetration, though execution depends on macroeconomic stabilization and policy reforms.
