### Risk Details

#### FX Translation and Earnings Drag
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** STC reports in SAR (stable/pegged to USD), while Telenor Pakistan generates revenue in PKR (depreciating). The combination of PKR volatility and STC's reporting currency will likely lead to significant non-cash translation losses and a drag on consolidated earnings, regardless of Telenor's local operational performance.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Implement aggressive currency hedging strategies
  - Utilize local PKR financing for CapEx to create a natural hedge

#### Double-Gate Regulatory Deadlock
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** The deal faces simultaneous high-friction regulatory hurdles: Saudi Arabia's CMA has a history of blocking outbound M&A (11+ deals), while Pakistan's telecom sector requires complex spectrum and antitrust approvals. The risk is that approval from one jurisdiction is contingent on terms that the other jurisdiction finds unacceptable.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Engage dual-track regulatory counsel in Riyadh and Islamabad
  - Secure pre-clearance from Saudi CMA before final binding offer

#### CapEx Inflation vs. Tech Ambition
- **Category:** CROSS_WORKSTREAM
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC intends to leverage its 5G expertise to modernize the target. However, the cost of importing high-tech network equipment (priced in USD/EUR) combined with PKR depreciation and Pakistan's high inflation will likely cause actual CapEx to exceed initial budgets significantly.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Build a 20-30% inflation buffer into the 5-year CapEx plan
  - Negotiate vendor financing in local currency where possible

#### Pakistan Political Instability
- **Category:** GEOPOLITICAL
- **Severity:** HIGH | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** A political stability score of 35/100 indicates high risk of sudden policy shifts, civil unrest, or changes in foreign ownership laws that could impair the asset's value.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Purchase Political Risk Insurance (PRI)
  - Maintain strong relationships with local government stakeholders

#### Capital Repatriation Complexity
- **Category:** CURRENCY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** HIGH
- **Description:** While repatriation is legally allowed under SBP regulations, the actual process is subject to strict compliance and potential delays due to Pakistan's foreign exchange reserve constraints.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Establish a clear repatriation schedule approved by SBP
  - Reinvest early profits into local growth to reduce immediate repatriation pressure

#### Antitrust and Spectrum Allocation
- **Category:** REGULATORY
- **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** Acquisition of a major player like Telenor may trigger antitrust scrutiny in Pakistan or lead to demands for spectrum redistribution by the regulator.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Proactive engagement with the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA)
  - Prepare a divestiture plan for non-core assets if required by antitrust regulators

#### Post-Merger Integration (PMI) Fatigue
- **Category:** COMPANY
- **Severity:** LOW | **Probability:** MEDIUM
- **Description:** STC has integrated 19 companies since inception. There is a risk of 'integration fatigue' or the application of a rigid Saudi corporate template to a volatile Pakistani market.
- **Mitigants:**
  - Adopt a 'light-touch' integration model for the first 24 months
  - Retain key Telenor Pakistan local management to preserve institutional knowledge

### Cross-Workstream Insights
- The most critical risk is the structural mismatch between STC's stable SAR reporting and Telenor's volatile PKR earnings, which creates a financial risk invisible when analyzing either company's balance sheet in isolation.
- There is a 'regulatory pincer' effect where the deal must survive both Saudi outbound restrictions and Pakistani inbound telecom regulations, increasing the probability of a deal collapse during the closing window.
- STC's strategic desire to deploy 5G (Buy-side) is fundamentally challenged by Pakistan's macroeconomic instability (Sell-side), meaning the cost of achieving the strategic objective is likely underestimated in the current valuation.
