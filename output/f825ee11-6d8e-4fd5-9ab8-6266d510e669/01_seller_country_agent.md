### Currency & Capital
The US operates a quasi-territorial tax system following the TCJA 2017. There are virtually no capital controls on the movement of money into or out of the country (Open Capital Account). GILTI (Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income) is used to tax foreign earnings and discourage offshore hoarding.

### Risk Flags
- TCJA Cliff: Phase-out of bonus depreciation (down to 20% by 2026) will increase effective corporate tax burdens.
- CFIUS Scrutiny: Heightened regulatory review for investments in AI, critical technology, and sensitive personal data.
- Antitrust Aggression: FTC and DOJ are actively challenging mergers that increase market concentration, favoring 'bolt-on' over 'mega-mergers'.
- Fiscal Pressure: High national debt servicing costs may limit future public investment.

### Narrative Summary
The US economy is transitioning from a period of high post-pandemic volatility to a state of normalization. GDP growth is projected to stabilize around 2.0%, while inflation is trending toward the Federal Reserve's 2% target, supporting a 'soft landing' scenario. For a high-growth tech entity like MongoDB, the primary macroeconomic tailwind is the integration of Generative AI into corporate productivity, though this is offset by a tightening regulatory environment.

From a deal perspective, the M&A landscape is shifting. While lower interest rates in 2025-2026 are expected to unlock private equity 'dry powder,' aggressive antitrust enforcement by the FTC and DOJ makes large-scale horizontal integration risky. Additionally, the 'TCJA Cliff' at the end of 2025 will likely increase the effective tax rate for corporations due to the expiration of capital expensing provisions, potentially impacting net cash flows for acquisitions.
