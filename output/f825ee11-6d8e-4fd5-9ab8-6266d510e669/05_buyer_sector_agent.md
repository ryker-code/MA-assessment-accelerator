### Narrative Summary
CoreWeave is evolving from a Specialized Cloud Provider (SCP) into a global 'AI Utility.' While it currently holds a dominant position in the non-hyperscaler GPUaaS market due to its NVIDIA partnership, it faces a critical strategic gap in vertical integration. Specifically, power and cooling have become the primary constraints to growth. The buyer needs exposure to the energy sector (SMRs, grid-edge technology) and specialized cooling infrastructure to secure the 'physical means of production' for its AI Factories. Additionally, to mitigate the regulatory risk of the 'NVIDIA Nexus' and the threat of hyperscaler chip independence, CoreWeave must diversify its hardware orchestration layer and expand its 'Sovereign AI' footprint in Europe to capture national-level compute demand. Acquisition of targets in energy, liquid cooling, or specialized AI networking would transform CoreWeave from a hardware-dependent lessor into a resilient, full-stack infrastructure powerhouse.
